How ChatGPT made my son laugh - and why it is important

By Lennart kiil
22/05/2023 - 3 minutes - 521 words

Recently I installed Marvin . Now, Marvin also happens to be the name of a dog dear to our family.

So my kids kind of feel strongly about the name, and were both a bit offended about an Artificial Intelligence with the same name.

But in this case Marvin is a

batteries-included library for building AI-powered software. Marvin’s job is to integrate AI directly into your codebase by making it look and feel like any other function. Marvin is building the gears, cranks, and pistons to let mechanical software systems integrate with and leverage the raw power of Large Language Models.

To test out this piece of software I asked it to construct various bots for me.

Marvin makes it easy to construct bots with different names, personalities and instructions.

I’ve found it difficult to get ChatGPT to be humorous. But I gave it a go again with Marvin after I asked it to construct a bot capable of making cringy dad jokes.

After constructing the bot I gave it this prompt:

Me: give me some tricks to make me son laugh with emojis

So, why am I writing a blog post about this?

Because I am interested in the intersection between Artificial Intelligence, language and “soul”.

I starting to think about this question:

How much does an AI need to know about us to make an ongoing interaction worthwhile?

Giving a bot a “personality” is one thing.

Revealing your own innermost life to the bot?

That is something else entirely.

But this is exactly what will start to happen. Now.
